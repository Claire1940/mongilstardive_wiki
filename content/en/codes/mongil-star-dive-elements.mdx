## Skill Investment and Character Roles

In the current 2026 meta, resource management is vital. You should not invest equally in every character's skills. Instead, categorize your roster into Main DPS, Sub-DPS, and Support roles to prioritize your materials. 

Main DPS characters, such as Fighters, Destroyers, and certain Assassins (like Esther or Flare), require significant on-field time. For these units, every skill—including their Basic Attack—should be maxed out to ensure consistent damage output. Conversely, Sub-DPS and Support units often rely on specific "hit-and-run" tactics. If a support character's buffs provide a fixed value that does not scale with level, such as Jiwon, it is often better to save your materials for characters with higher damage multipliers.

| Character Role | On-Field Time | Skill Priority | Investment Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Main DPS** | High | All Skills + Basic ATK | **Maximum** |
| **Sub-DPS** | Medium/Short | High Multiplier Skills | **High** |
| **Support** | Low | Buff/Debuff Scaling Skills | **Selective** |
| **Tank/Utility** | Situational | Survival & Mitigation Skills | **Moderate** |

> **💡 Tip:** Before spending rare materials, check if a Support character's buff scales with skill level. If the buff is fixed, only upgrade the skill if you need the extra damage multiplier.

## Maximizing Mongil Star Dive Elements through Equipment

Equipment in 2026 is categorized by rarity, with 4-star and 5-star gear offering powerful Set Effects. The most critical factor when choosing gear is matching the equipment’s effect element with your character’s innate **mongil star dive elements**. A mismatch here often results in a significant loss of potential DPS or utility.

Beyond elemental matching, you must pay attention to the trigger conditions. Some sets activate on a Basic Attack, while others require specific actions like a "Tag-out Skill" or a "Support Skill."

| Skill Type | Activation Condition | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Switch Skill** | Activates automatically upon entering the field. | Burst damage and entry buffs. |
| **Tag-out Skill** | Activates automatically when the character is swapped out. | Leaving behind debuffs or shields. |
| **Support Skill** | Activates under specific conditions; character retreats after use. | Off-field healing or elemental application. |

### Role Proficiency and the "Fourth Piece"
While the first three pieces of equipment generally focus on offensive stats like Crit Rate and ATK, the fourth piece is specialized. It features a **Role Proficiency** stat that only provides a benefit if it matches the character's class. For example, equipping a piece with "Assassin Proficiency" on a Destroyer is inefficient, as the Destroyer class specifically requires "Neutralization DMG Boost" rather than "Suppression DMG Boost."

## Monsterling Synergy and Link Chains

Monsterlings are more than just pets; they are essential extensions of your character's kit. The first slot in your Monsterling UI is the most important, as only the creature equipped here can be summoned to the battlefield using a **Link Chain**. The secondary slots provide passive stat boosts but do not allow for active summoning.

Following the major update on April 23rd, 2026, all Monsterlings have been standardized to **ATK-type stats**, simplifying the build process. However, you must still align the Monsterling's Rank Effect with your character's specific **mongil star dive elements** to maximize effectiveness.

### Choosing the Right Traits
When filtering for Monsterling traits, always prioritize those that complement your character's class-specific damage boost:
*   **Fighters:** Look for "Brawls DMG Boost."
*   **Destroyers:** Focus on "Neutralization DMG Boost."
*   **Assassins:** Seek out "Suppression DMG Boost."
*   **Supports:** Prioritize "Support DMG Boost" and "Skill Cooldown Reduction."

### The Mutation System
Some of the most powerful Link Chain Monsterlings can only be obtained through **Mutation**. This requires fusing specific base Monsterlings, often involving a "Mutant" variant as a material. Tools like the Tychara Fusion Tool or Zeroluck Fusion Calculator are indispensable for players looking to unlock these rare endgame summons in 2026.

## Enhancing Strength with Food Buffs

Never underestimate the power of a well-cooked meal. Food buffs are active in all modes, including the challenging Conquest and Dimensional Rift stages. High-grade side dishes are particularly useful for increasing **Capture Proficiency**, which allows your Nyanko to tame higher-level bosses. To expand your culinary repertoire, complete Guidebook quests or visit specific NPCs in the main hub to purchase new recipes.

## FAQ

**Q: How do mongil star dive elements affect my damage output?**

A: Matching the element of your equipment's set effect and your Monsterling's rank effect to your character's innate element provides significant percentage-based damage bonuses. Using mismatched elements often results in wasted stat slots and lower overall DPS in endgame content.

**Q: What is the most efficient way to level up my Nyanko?**

A: You should farm every boss and monster on the map daily, as they only respawn after the daily reset. This consistent farming is the only way to reach the high Nyanko levels required to activate Link Chains for endgame bosses.

**Q: Should I buy the Monsterling Chest or the Equipment Chest from the Conquest Shop?**

A: In 2026, it is highly recommended to save your tokens for the **Equipment Chest** unlocked at Level 10. The Monsterling Chest has a high chance of dropping low-grade creatures, whereas the Equipment Chest guarantees rare materials needed for 5-star gear crafting.

**Q: Why isn't my Destroyer character getting a damage boost from Assassin Proficiency?**

A: Damage boosts from Role Proficiency are class-specific. Assassin Proficiency only converts into Suppression DMG, which Destroyers cannot utilize. Destroyers require Neutralization DMG Boost to see a tangible increase in their combat performance.
