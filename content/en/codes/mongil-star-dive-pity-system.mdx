## How the Soft Pity System Works

The **mongil star dive pity system** introduces a "Soft Pity" mechanic starting at the 75th pull. From pull 1 through 74, your chance of hitting a top-tier character remains a flat 1%. Once you hit the 75th summon without a 5-star result, the game begins to incrementally increase the percentage chance for every subsequent pull.

By the time you reach pull 80 or 85, the probability is substantially higher than the base rate. If you are extremely unlucky and do not trigger a 5-star through the boosted rates, the 90th pull acts as the "Hard Pity," providing a 100% guaranteed high-rarity drop. This 90-pull ceiling is lower than many other open-world gacha games, making the path to obtaining characters like Esther or Mina more attainable for the average player.

## Monster Training and Synergy

A unique aspect that differentiates this game from titles like *Genshin Impact* or *Honkai: Star Rail* is the Monster Wing system. While the **mongil star dive pity system** governs your character acquisition, your power level is equally dependent on the monsters you collect and train.

These monsters do not fight permanently alongside you like in *Palworld*. Instead, they function as "Link Chains" or "Keychains" that provide passive stat boosts and active skill triggers. You can combine different Monster Wings to create specialized builds for your three-person party. When summoning for characters, consider how their element (such as Mina’s Fire-based clones) will synergize with your current monster collection.

### Dimensional Rift and Conquest Modes

To earn the currency required for summoning, players must engage with two primary end-game modes:
1.  **Dimensional Rift:** A time-attack mode where you clear waves of monsters across five stages. Higher scores are achieved by using "Bonus Characters" listed at the top of the UI, which can provide score multipliers of 20% or more.
2.  **Conquest:** A boss-rush mode where you face off against powerful entities encountered during the main story. This mode is the primary source for character ascension materials and high-tier Monster Wings.

## FAQ

**Q: Does pity carry over between banners in Mongil Star Dive?**

A: Yes, the **mongil star dive pity system** carries over between banners of the same type. For example, if you perform 50 pulls on Esther's limited banner and it ends, your 50-pull progress will transfer to the next limited character banner.

**Q: What happens if I pull a 5-star character before 75 pulls?**

A: If you pull a 5-star character at any point, your pity counter immediately resets to zero. The soft pity only exists to increase your chances if you have been unsuccessful up to that point.

**Q: Are the "Monster Wings" part of the character gacha?**

A: No, Monster Wings are primarily obtained through gameplay, boss drops in Conquest mode, and specific in-game events. The main gacha system is focused on human and demi-human characters.

**Q: Is there a weapon gacha in Mongil Star Dive?**

A: As of the April 2026 launch, the focus is on the character summon system and the artifact system. Many powerful weapons and artifacts are tied to character-specific milestones or high-level boss loot rather than a separate "weapon-only" banner.
