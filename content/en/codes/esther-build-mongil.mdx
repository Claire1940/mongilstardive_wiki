## Understanding the Pity System

One of the most player-friendly aspects of Mongil Star Dive in 2026 is the carry-over pity system. Understanding how this works is vital for managing your pulls for an **esther build mongil**.

*   **Soft Pity:** Starts at **76 pulls**. Your chances of hitting a five-star increase significantly here.
*   **Hard Pity:** Guaranteed five-star at **90 pulls**.
*   **Carry-over:** If you do not pull Esther on her banner, your progress carries over to the next limited banner (likely Mina or Nara).

This means you can safely "test the waters" on Esther’s banner. If you happen to get her early, you have a top-tier Wind DPS. If not, you are simply building a guarantee for the next meta-defining unit.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Esther better than Flare for beginners?**

A: Both are excellent, but they serve different roles. Esther is fantastic for general mob clearing and Wind-weak stages, while Flare excels at single-target boss DPS. Your choice should depend on which supports (Chiwon or Nara/Mina) you plan to pull for.

**Q: What is the most important stat for an esther build mongil?**

A: Focus on Attack Power and Wind Damage Bonus. Since Esther relies on her high-frequency hits to maintain Wind Affliction, increasing her raw damage per hit is the most effective way to scale her performance.

**Q: Does the pity carry over if the banner ends?**

A: Yes, the pity on limited banners carries over to the next limited banner. If you are at 80 pulls and Esther's banner ends, you will still be at 80 pulls when the next character (like Nara) arrives.

**Q: Can I play Esther without Chiwon?**

A: You can, but it is not optimal. Chiwon provides the specific buffs and elemental resonance that make Esther truly shine. If you don't have Chiwon, consider using a high-tier four-star Wind support until you can pull a better replacement.
