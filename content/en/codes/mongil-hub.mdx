## Detailed Character Analysis: Giwan vs. Flare

Choosing between the top two targets in the **mongil hub** recruitment pool depends largely on your long-term plans.

### Giwan: The Support Powerhouse
Giwan is widely considered the best long-term investment. As a support character, her longevity in the meta is almost guaranteed. Her "Divine Harmony" skill not only boosts Wind damage—which perfectly complements limited characters like Esther—but also decreases stamina consumption. In a game where movement and ability uptime are tied to stamina, this utility is invaluable for high-level raids and competitive play.

### Flare: The Boss Slayer
If your goal is to clear content as fast as possible, Flare is your ideal target. She excels in Fire-element teams and has a kit specifically designed to melt boss health bars. While supports like Giwan offer utility, Flare provides the raw power needed to bypass gear checks in the early stages of the 2026 campaign.

> **💡 Tip:** Many players choose to reroll for Giwan on the Beginner Banner and then use their 50-pull selector (available later) to grab Flare, ensuring a balanced team of both top-tier support and DPS.

## Advanced Reroll Tactics and Emulator Usage

As of 2026, many players have attempted to use emulators to speed up the process at the **mongil hub**. However, developers have implemented several blocks on common emulation software. If you find your client crashing or refusing to load, you may need to perform rerolls one instance at a time on a mobile device or a verified PC client.

### Using "Spools" and Threads
During your summons, you will notice a secondary currency accumulating: Spools (or Threads). These are often overlooked by beginners but are vital for min-maxing.

*   **Standard Recruitment:** Yields basic threads used for upgrade materials.
*   **Special Recruitment:** Yields spools that can be traded for limited-run Artifacts or even additional summons on the rate-up banner.

If you are a "sweaty" player looking to maximize your account, you can technically use these threads to buy a few extra pulls for limited characters like Esther, though the 1% success rate makes this a high-effort, low-reward strategy compared to the guaranteed 5-star on the beginner banner.

## FAQ

**Q: How long does it take to reach the Mongil Hub for the first time?**

A: On a fresh account, it takes approximately 10 minutes of active gameplay to complete the tutorial and unlock the **mongil hub** summoning menus. Skipping cutscenes can reduce this time slightly.

**Q: Can I reroll for limited characters like Esther?**

A: While possible, it is considered "unviable" for most players. You only get about 20 general summons at the start, which do not work on limited banners. You would need to play for over an hour to earn enough currency for a meaningful number of limited pulls, or rely on the very low amount of "Spools" converted from general pulls.

**Q: What happens if I delete my account to try again?**

A: As mentioned in the guide, deleting your account via the settings menu will lock that account/ID for 30 days. To reroll effectively, you must use different email addresses or "salted" emails if your provider allows it (e.g., email+1@gmail.com), though some systems in 2026 have begun filtering these.

**Q: Is there a pity system in Mongil Star Dive?**

A: Yes, there is a soft pity that typically starts around the 76th pull on limited banners. However, for the initial reroll at the **mongil hub**, you are relying on the Beginner Banner's guarantee, which triggers much earlier (within 20 pulls).
