Many chests in the 2026 version of Mongil: Star Dive are locked behind light-based or elemental puzzles. In Hananis, you will frequently encounter "Star Pedestals."

*   **Elemental Matching:** Strike the pedestal with the element matching its glow (Blue for Water, Red for Fire).

## FAQ

**Q: Do mongil star dive chests respawn after I open them?**

A: Standard wooden crates and some common chests in the open world respawn every 24 to 48 hours. However, Gilded trunks, Star-Touched relics, and chests tied to specific puzzles or "First Clear" achievements are one-time rewards and do not respawn.

**Q: I found a chest but I can't open it. What am I missing?**

A: Some **mongil star dive chests** are "Combat Locked." You must defeat all enemies in the immediate vicinity (usually indicated by a red circle on the ground or a barrier) before the chest becomes interactable. Other chests may require a specific elemental key or character level.

**Q: Are the rewards in Hananis chests the same for everyone?**

A: While the *type* of loot (e.g., Gold, Ore, Gear) is fixed, the specific rarity and stats of the items are often randomized based on your current World Level. Higher World Levels in 2026 yield significantly better rewards from the same chest locations.

**Q: Where can I find the rarest mongil star dive chests in the early game?**

A: The rarest chests in the early game are typically found in the "Hananis Ancient Ruins" sub-zone. These require solving complex light-beam puzzles and often contain the most valuable upgrade materials for your starting weapons.
