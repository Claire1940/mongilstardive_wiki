## Understanding Monsterling Basics and Capture Proficiency

Before diving into the advanced mechanics of combining, every player must master the fundamentals of Monsterling growth. Growth is primarily driven by "Chow," a specific resource dropped from Monsterling Enhancement Training stages found within the Paths menu. Running these stages and utilizing the auto-clear function is the most efficient way to burn stamina for experience gains.

Your ability to interact with higher-tier creatures is dictated by your Capture Proficiency. This stat acts as a gatekeeper; if your proficiency is too low, you simply cannot capture high-level monsters in the wild. Alongside this, the "High Grade Drop Chance" determines the quality of essences you receive upon defeating enemies.

| Stat | Function | How to Improve |
|---|---|---|
| **Capture Proficiency** | Determines the level of monsters you can catch. | Leveling up your main character and progression. |
| **High Grade Drop Chance** | Increases the rarity of dropped Monsterling essences. | Gear, specific character buffs, and progression. |
| **Monsterling Level** | Increases base stats of the creature. | Using **Chow** from Enhancement Training. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check your Capture Proficiency before heading into high-level zones. There is nothing worse than finding a rare spawn you lack the stats to capture.

## Mastering the Combining System

Combining is the primary method for free-to-play players to increase the rarity of their Monsterlings. When you combine two units, the result will always retain the highest level of the two parents. For example, merging a level 20 Monsterling with a level 1 unit ensures the resulting creature is level 20.

The most critical element of combining is the "Use Material Traits" toggle. When this is selected, the game will randomly pick two traits from the parent pool to pass down to the new Monsterling. If you uncheck this box, the new unit will receive entirely randomized traits, which is generally discouraged once you have secured high-quality stats like Critical Damage or Skill Cooldown reduction.

### Rarity Upgrade Probabilities

When combining, there is a statistical chance that the resulting Monsterling will "rank up" to the next rarity tier. Understanding these percentages helps you decide when to risk a combine and when to wait for better materials.

| Current Grade | Success (Stay Same) | Upgrade (Next Tier) | Skip (Two Tiers) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gray (1-Star)** | 80% | 20% | 0% |
| **Green (2-Star)** | 75% | 25% | 1% (to Purple) |
| **Blue (3-Star)** | 70% | 30% | 1% (to Legendary) |
| **Purple (4-Star)** | 70% | 30% | N/A |

## Effective Mongil Star Dive Storage Management

As your collection grows, your **mongil star dive storage** can become overwhelming. To prevent accidentally consuming valuable units during batch combines, you must utilize the locking system. Any Monsterling with desirable traits—such as a 15% Critical Damage boost—should be locked immediately.

Furthermore, pay attention to the icons in the corner of your Monsterling portraits. These indicate "Link Chains." A grayed-out icon means you have not unlocked that specific link yet, while numbered icons (1, 2, etc.) show the current level of the link. Keeping units required for link chains in your **mongil star dive storage** is vital for maximizing your team's passive bonuses.

### Inventory Cleanup Strategy
1.  **Lock High-Value Traits:** Any unit with Purple or Gold traits should be protected.
2.  **Identify Link Materials:** Check the Link Chain menu and lock required units.
3.  **Batch Combine Low Tiers:** Use the batch combine tool for Gray and Green units to clear space and fish for Blue rarity upgrades.
4.  **Filter by Grade:** Regularly sort your storage to identify fodder versus keepers.

## Advanced Min-Maxing with Pure Purées

For end-game players, the standard combining system eventually becomes too risky. This is where "Pure Purées" come into play. These premium items allow you to lock up to three specific stats during a combine. If you have a Purple Monsterling with three perfect traits, using 20 Pure Purées ensures that even if the combine fails to rank up, you keep those specific stats.

If the combine is successful and you reach Legendary (Gold) status, you are guaranteed to keep your three locked stats, and the game will add one random fourth stat. To perfect this fourth slot, you would then merge that unit with another Legendary, locking the original three and the desired fourth stat from the second unit.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Never uncheck "Use Material Traits" when working with Blue rarity or higher. Doing so can "brick" a Monsterling by replacing top-tier stats with low-tier garbage.

## Elite and Boss Monsterling Progression

Elite Monsterlings like Custos, Aamon of Arden, and Duozini follow a stricter set of rules because they are much harder to obtain. These units are often limited by Conquest entry tickets, making every drop precious.

The recommended path for Elites is to combine them with "normal" Monsterlings only until they reach Blue rarity. Once an Elite is Blue, you should only ever combine it with a duplicate of itself or a specific unit required for its Link Chain. Combining an Elite with a duplicate guarantees the result remains that specific Elite (100% chance), whereas combining it with a random unit introduces a 50% risk of losing the Elite species entirely.

| Monsterling Type | Best Fodder (to Blue) | Best Fodder (Blue to Gold) |
|---|---|---|
| **Common (Slimes, etc.)** | Any same-grade unit | Any same-grade unit |
| **Elite (Aamon, etc.)** | Common units | **Duplicates Only** |
| **Boss (Custos, etc.)** | Common units | **Duplicates Only** |

## FAQ

**Q: How do I increase my mongil star dive storage capacity?**

A: Storage can typically be expanded using premium currency (Gems) or through specific account level milestones. Regularly cleaning out 1-star and 2-star fodder via batch combining is the best way to manage space without spending currency.

**Q: What are Upgrade Stones and should I use them?**

A: Upgrade Stones are rare items that allow you to bypass the RNG of combining to increase a Monsterling's rarity. Because they are difficult to obtain (often found in the Fate Exchange or premium shops), you should save them exclusively for Elite or Boss Monsterlings that have perfect traits.

**Q: Can I lose my Monsterling's level during a combine?**

A: No. The resulting Monsterling will always inherit the highest level between the two units used in the combination. You can safely use a level 1 unit as fodder for your level 20 unit.

**Q: What is the maximum value for traits like Critical Damage?**

A: Based on current 2026 data, the maximum value for a Critical Damage trait on a standard Monsterling is 15%. If you find a unit with this stat, lock it immediately regardless of its current rarity.
