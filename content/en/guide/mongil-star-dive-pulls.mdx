The summoning system in Mongil: Star Dive revolves around two primary currencies: Starlight Crystals and Compasses of Destiny. Currently, the base rate for a 5-star character sits at approximately **1%**. While this may seem low compared to some contemporary titles, the game compensates by offering guaranteed pity milestones and specific banner mechanics that favor the player over long summoning sessions.

## The Importance of Character Advancements

Getting a single copy of a 5-star character is only the beginning. To truly compete in the endgame, you need to navigate the advancement system. Every time you receive a duplicate during your **mongil star dive pulls**, you gain materials to "ascend" or "advance" that character, unlocking passive traits and stat boosts that are otherwise unattainable.

For example, a G1 at base level is a solid attacker, but at Advancement Level 2 (A2), her ability to cycle through skills increases significantly. This makes "dupe hunting" a core part of the summoning strategy for mid-to-late game players.

### Advancement Priority Table

| Character | Ideal Advancement | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Esther** | **A1** | Significant boost to support aura range. |
| **G1** | **A2** | Unlocks critical damage scaling for Wind skills. |
| **Daisy** | **A5 (Max)** | Transforms this common 4-star into a viable budget DPS. |
| **Raina** | **A3** | Enhances crowd control duration for arena matches. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not spread your Starlight Crystals across too many banners. It is better to have one A2 character than three A0 characters if you plan on clearing high-difficulty raids.

## Strategies for Starlight Crystal Management

Managing your resources is the difference between a top-tier account and one that plateaus early. In Mongil: Star Dive, the cost of summons is relatively high, meaning every failed multi-pull hurts your long-term progress.

1.  **Check for Free Codes:** Netmarble frequently releases promotional codes that provide free Starlight Crystals and Compasses of Destiny. Always check the official [Netmarble Mongil Site](https://www.netmarble.com) for the latest community events.
2.  **The "Bait" Rule:** If a banner features a character that doesn't fit your primary elemental team, skip it. Even if the character is "juicy" or aesthetically pleasing, your resources are better spent on units that provide synergy.
3.  **Artifact vs. Character Balance:** Be aware that some banners have higher artifact rates. If your goal is to expand your roster, avoid banners that heavily weight artifacts over playable units.

## Handling the "Daisy" Curse

If you spend any significant time on **mongil star dive pulls**, you will inevitably encounter the "Daisy Curse." Daisy is a 4-star Elf character who appears with high frequency. While it can be frustrating to see her animation repeatedly when you're hunting for Esther, a max-advanced Daisy is surprisingly competent for early-game progression and specific resource farming stages. 

Instead of getting frustrated by 4-star duplicates, view them as the foundation of your secondary teams. Characters like Raina and Daisy, when fully advanced, can often outperform a base-level 5-star in niche scenarios.

## FAQ

**Q: What are the odds of getting a 5-star in Mongil: Star Dive?**

A: The standard rate for a 5-star character is 1%. However, featured banners often have a "guaranteed" pity system where you are assured a 5-star unit after a set number of pulls (typically 80-100).

**Q: Should I pull for Esther or wait for the next banner?**

A: If you are building a Wind team, Esther is an essential unit. However, if your current team is built around Fire or Water, Esther might be a "bait" banner for you, and you should save your crystals for a unit that matches your elemental core.

**Q: Are artifacts worth keeping from my mongil star dive pulls?**

A: Yes, artifacts provide essential stat boosts and unique passives. While characters are more exciting, a high-tier artifact can often be the difference between winning and losing in the Arena.

**Q: What is the "yellow glimmer" everyone talks about?**

A: In the summoning animation, a yellow or golden light indicates that you have successfully pulled a 5-star (SSR) character or high-tier artifact. It is the most sought-after visual cue in the game.
