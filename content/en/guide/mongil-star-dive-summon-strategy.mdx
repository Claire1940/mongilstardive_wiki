## Understanding the Banner and Pity System

Before pulling, you must understand the underlying mechanics of the gacha system. In 2026, the pity system is remarkably player-friendly compared to other titles in the genre, but it still requires disciplined resource management.

| Feature | Detail | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hard Pity** | 90 Pulls | Guaranteed 5-star character |
| **Soft Pity** | Starts at 76 Pulls | Significantly increased 5-star rates |
| **Pity Carry-over** | Yes | Progress transfers to the next limited banner |
| **Selector Banner** | 50 Pulls | Guaranteed choice of one standard 5-star |

> **💡 Tip:** Since pity carries over between limited banners, you can "build pity" on a current banner if you want the 4-stars, but be careful not to trigger your 5-star guarantee if you are saving for a future T0 unit.

The "New Friends" banner is your first major milestone. It allows you to select a specific 5-star and guarantees them within 50 wishes. Most veteran players recommend targeting either **Flare** for boss DPS or **Chiwon** for elite-tier support.

## Optimizing Your Mongil Star Dive Summon Strategy

Your summoning path should be determined by your first 5-star pull or your selector choice. Currently, the meta is split between two primary elemental cores: the Wind Team and the Fire Team.

### The Wind Team: Esther and Chiwon
If you choose Chiwon from your selector, your primary objective should be pulling for **Esther**. Esther serves as the premier Wind DPS at launch. When paired with Chiwon’s wind affliction and Sangun’s crowd control, this team offers high sustain and exceptional area-of-effect (AoE) damage.

### The Fire Team: Flare and the Future Meta
For those who prefer explosive single-target damage, starting with **Flare** is the way to go. However, the current **mongil star dive summon strategy** for Fire players actually involves *skipping* the Esther banner. Flare synergizes perfectly with two upcoming units: **Mina** and **Nara**. 

Nara, in particular, is considered a "must-have" support. Although she is an Ice element character, her "bubble" mechanic deals massive bonus damage when popped by Fire attacks. This makes her the backbone of the Fire meta well into late 2026.

## Character Spotlight: Sangun (The Wind Tiger)

Sangun is a 5-star Wind Fighter who often appears as a "spook" on banners or a secondary choice. While not as flashy as Esther, Sangun is a pillar of stability for Wind-based compositions. Her ability to gather enemies makes her invaluable for clearing timed dungeons.

### Sangun Skill Breakdown

| Skill Name | Effect | Strategic Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tiger Strike** | Spins and hurls Tiger's Howl | Pulls enemies to a central point |
| **Fearsome Tiger** | Leaping strike | Gap closer and secondary pull |
| **Cataclysm (ULT)** | Summon Wind Tiger | High AoE damage + Wind Affliction |
| **Aura of Harmony** | Wild Fervor passive | Increases attack speed by 20% after counters |

Sangun thrives when you actively control her to land successful counters. The "Wild Fervor" buff significantly boosts her DPS, and her signature artifact, **"Held Close by Nature,"** provides an unconditional Crit Rate boost that makes her a formidable sub-DPS.

## Upcoming Banners to Watch

Based on the 2026 roadmap and beta data, two characters are expected to shift the meta significantly. Planning your pulls around these two is vital for long-term account health.

### 1. Nara (The T0 Support)
Nara is an Ice Support who provides shielding, knockbacks, and airborne CC. Her most unique trait is her bubble field. These bubbles pop for extra Ice damage normally, but if hit with Fire damage (from someone like Flare), the explosion damage is multiplied. She is widely considered the best support in the game at launch.

### 2. Mina (The Fire DPS)
Mina is the classic "fox girl" archetype, utilizing fox-fire orbs and clones. She provides consistent Fire Affliction, which lowers enemy resistance. If you are building a Fire team, Mina provides the sustained AoE damage that Flare lacks, creating a perfect synergy for multi-target boss fights.

## The Reroll Strategy: Is It Worth It?

Because the tutorial takes approximately 30 minutes, rerolling in Mongil: Star Dive is a time-consuming process. However, for those seeking the perfect start, the goal is to land a 5-star from the standard pool within the first few free pulls, then use the selector to complete a synergy.

1. **Target 1:** Pull Flare or Chiwon from the initial free currency.
2. **Target 2:** Use the 50-pull selector to get the one you didn't pull.
3. **Target 3:** Save all remaining Compasses for Nara or Mina.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not delete your account to reroll if you are using a primary email. There is a 30-day cooldown on account deletion. Use guest accounts or salted emails instead.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I pull for Esther if I have Flare?**

A: If you are focusing on a Fire team, Esther is a safe skip. While she is strong, your resources are better spent on Nara, who provides the specific synergy Flare needs to dominate the endgame boss meta.

**Q: How does the pity carry-over work in the 2026 update?**

A: If you do 50 pulls on Esther’s banner and don't get a 5-star, those 50 pulls count toward the 90-pull pity on the next limited banner (e.g., Nara). This is a core part of a smart **mongil star dive summon strategy**.

**Q: What is the best 5-star to take from the New Friends selector?**

A: Flare is generally the best choice for players who want to focus on high-end bossing. Chiwon is the better choice for players who want a versatile support that fits into almost any team composition.

**Q: Is Sangun a good replacement for Esther?**

A: Sangun is more of a sub-DPS and crowd-controller. While she can lead a team, she lacks the raw damage output Esther provides. However, in a Wind team, they are actually better together than as replacements for one another.
