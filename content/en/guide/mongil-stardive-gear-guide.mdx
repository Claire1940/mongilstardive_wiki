## Equipment Basics: Slots and Main Stats

In Mongil: Star Dive, every character has four primary equipment slots: Headgear, Chestpieces, Gloves, and Footwear. While the substats are randomized upon acquisition or crafting, the main stats for the first three slots are fixed. Footwear is the "make or break" piece for any build due to its unique proficiency mechanic.

| Equipment Type | Fixed Main Stat | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Headgear** | HP | Vital for survivability in high-level Conquest stages. |
| **Chestpieces** | Defense | Reduces incoming damage; essential for Destroyers and Tanks. |
| **Gloves** | Attack | The primary source of raw flat damage for all roles. |
| **Footwear** | **Role Proficiency** | **CRITICAL:** Only works if the role matches the character. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always check the Role Proficiency on your Footwear. If you equip "Fighter Proficiency" boots on an "Assassin" character like Esther, the main stat will provide zero benefit to that character.

## The Best 5-Star Elemental Sets

Once you unlock high-level crafting and Conquest mode, you should aim for 5-star elemental sets. These provide massive 2-piece and 4-piece bonuses that align with specific combat triggers like Special Skills, Crits, or elemental afflictions.

### Abyss Set (Wind)
The Abyss set is currently the gold standard for Wind carries. It provides a 10% Wind DMG buff for 5 seconds upon using a Special Skill (2-set) and another 10% Wind DMG buff upon landing a critical hit (4-set). This is the perfect match for Esther, especially when paired with her "Dust Cloud" zone.

### Arbiter Set (Ice)
Designed for Ice fighters like Ophelia, the Arbiter set focuses on Switch Skills. It grants Ice DMG buffs when swapping characters and attacking enemies already suffering from Ice Affliction. This rewards a high-intensity playstyle where you constantly rotate your party.

### Hunnel’s Roar (Fire)
For Fire teams, Hunnel’s Roar is the premier choice. It buffs Fire damage and Critical damage based on the enemy's affliction status. It is highly recommended for Flare, as she excels at applying Fire affliction and shredding enemy defenses.

## Artifact Recommendations for Top Characters

Artifacts in Mongil: Star Dive function differently than standard armor. They are divided into four classes: Assassin, Destroyer, Fighter, and Support. When selecting an artifact, you must look at the "Trigger Condition" rather than just the star rating. A 4-star artifact with a trigger you can activate 100% of the time is better than a 5-star artifact with a condition your team cannot meet.

| Character | Role | Recommended Artifact | Key Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Esther** | Assassin | **Wind Harboring Broom** | Buffs ATK and Crit DMG for Wind carries. |

| **Ophelia** | Fighter | **Subzero Verdict** | Enhances combo chains and skill damage. |

## How to Craft and Optimize Your Gear

The crafting system is unlocked early in Episode 1 via the "Do It Yourself!" side quest. To succeed in the mid-to-late game, you must transition from "filler" gear to optimized sets.

1.  **Gather Materials:** Use your 5 daily Requests to target specific monsters for materials. For 5-star materials, you must challenge the **Conquest** bosses.
2.  **The Level 3 Rule:** 5-star gear can have up to 4 substats. You see two at Level 1 and a third at Level 3. If the first three substats are poor (e.g., flat HP on a DPS), stop enhancing and use it as fodder.
3.  **Prioritize Footwear:** As soon as crafting is unlocked, focus on getting Footwear that matches your main DPS's role.
4.  **Mining:** Rare minerals like Rubies and Sapphires are often hidden in the corners of maps. Don't just rush to the objective; explore the environment.

> **💡 Tip:** When using enhanced equipment as material for a new piece, you recover approximately 70% of the experience. Don't be afraid to level up 4-star gear early on to clear difficult content.

## Common Gear Mistakes to Avoid

Many players struggle in the first few weeks of 2026 because they treat Mongil: Star Dive like a standard stat-check game. Success requires understanding the "Monster Link" and "Swap" mechanics.

*   **Ignoring Off-Field Value:** Characters like Giwan or Francis provide value even when they aren't the active character. Equip them with artifacts like *Melody Played on Heartstrings* to ensure that every time you swap your DPS back in, they have a massive damage multiplier.
*   **Obsessing Over Set Bonuses Early:** In the very early game, a piece of gear with a high Attack main stat is better than a weak piece that completes a set. Focus on filling all slots first, then refine your sets.
*   **Neglecting Resistance Shred:** 4-star sets like *Mountain Peak Majesty* provide resistance shred. Even if you have 5-star gear, sometimes a support character is better off wearing a 4-star set that reduces enemy resistance to help your main carry deal more damage.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I get 5-star monster materials for the Abyss set?**

A: These materials are primarily obtained through the **Conquest** mode. As you defeat higher-stage bosses, you unlock the ability to exchange boss souls for specific 5-star materials or completed gear pieces.

**Q: Does the mongil stardive gear guide recommend 4-star gear for the endgame?**

A: While 5-star gear is the ultimate goal, certain 4-star sets like *Moonshadow* (for crit utility) and *Mountain Peak Majesty* (for resistance shred) remain viable for support characters well into the mid-game.

**Q: Why isn't my character's damage increasing after equipping new boots?**

A: Check the **Role Proficiency** on the boots. If the role (Fighter, Assassin, Destroyer, Support) does not match your character's role, the main stat of the footwear is disabled.

**Q: How many substats can 5-star equipment have?**

A: 5-star equipment can have a maximum of 4 substats. Two are visible at Level 1, and additional slots or enhancements occur at Levels 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, and 18.
