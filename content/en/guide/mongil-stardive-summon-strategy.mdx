## The Core Mongil Stardive Summon Strategy: Pity and Rates

Before diving into specific characters, you must understand the underlying math of the gacha system. Mongil: Star Dive utilizes a standardized pity system that is relatively generous compared to other modern titles, especially since pity progress carries over between limited banners.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Hard Pity** | **90 Pulls** (Guaranteed 5-star) |
| **Soft Pity** | Starts at **76 Pulls** (Increased rates) |
| **Currency** | Compasses |
| **Banner Carry-over** | Yes (Progress transfers to the next limited banner) |
| **New Friends Banner** | Guaranteed 5-star of choice within **50 Pulls** |

> **💡 Tip:** Since pity carries over, you can safely "build pity" or test your luck on a current banner like Esther's. If you don't hit the 5-star early, those pulls still count toward the next high-tier unit like Nara.

Approximately 10 to 15 minutes into the game, you will unlock the "New Friends" selector banner. This is the most important part of your early **mongil stardive summon strategy**. You have six primary choices: Ophelia, Flare, Penny, Chiwon, Gabi, and Sangun.

While all these characters are viable, two stand out as the objective "best" picks for long-term account health:

1.  **Flare (Fire DPS):** Widely considered the best boss killer in the game. She is a ranged fire attacker who applies fire affliction, making her essential for endgame boss encounters.
2.  **Chiwon (Wind Support):** The premier wind support who provides massive utility and synergy for wind-based teams.

## Analyzing Current and Future Banners

The launch meta is currently dominated by **Esther**, a powerful Wind DPS. However, veteran players from the beta (CBT) know that two massive power-creepers are on the horizon: **Mina** and **Nara**.

### Esther: To Pull or To Skip?
Esther is a top-tier Wind DPS who excels at applying wind affliction. If you selected **Chiwon** from your beginner banner, Esther is a natural fit. Together, they create a synergistic wind core that can carry you through the majority of the 2026 story content.

### Nara: The T0 Support
Nara is an Ice Support who was widely regarded as the "best support at launch" during the beta. Her kit includes:
*   **Ice Bubbles:** Deals ice damage and creates an area that buffs ranged character damage.
*   **Elemental Reactions:** If you hit her bubbles with Fire damage (from Flare or Mina), they pop for massive bonus damage.
*   **Crowd Control:** Her ultimate features airborne and knockback effects, providing timeless utility.

### Mina: The Fire Fox
Mina is a Fire DPS known for her "fox fire" archetype. She summons clones and applies fire affliction, making her the perfect partner for Flare in a mono-fire or dual-element fire/ice team.

## Team Synergy Paths

Your **mongil stardive summon strategy** should revolve around building a 3-man team that shares elemental or mechanical synergies. Because you can only field three characters at once, every slot is precious.

### Path A: The Fire/Ice Powerhouse (Recommended)
This path focuses on long-term scaling and boss shredding.
*   **Step 1:** Select **Flare** from the beginner banner.
*   **Step 2:** Skip or do minimal pulls on Esther.
*   **Step 3:** Save all Compasses for **Nara** (Support) and **Mina** (DPS).
*   **Result:** A team of Flare, Nara, and Mina provides the highest burst damage potential in the current 2026 meta.

### Path B: The Wind Gale
This path is easier to build immediately upon launch.
*   **Step 1:** Select **Chiwon** from the beginner banner.
*   **Step 2:** Pull for **Esther** on the limited banner.
*   **Step 3:** Slot in **Sangun** or a 4-star healer/utility unit.
*   **Result:** A highly cohesive wind team that excels at AoE and sustained DPS.

## Reroll Guide for the Perfect Start

If you have the patience, rerolling can jumpstart your account by giving you two 5-star units before you even finish the first chapter.

1.  **Login as Guest:** Do not link your account immediately.
2.  **Complete Tutorial:** Play until you unlock the recruitment function (approx. 30 minutes).
3.  **Claim Rewards:** Collect all pre-registration rewards and mail items (look for Francis and Compasses).
4.  **Spend Compasses:** Pull on the Limited Banner (Esther) first.
5.  **Check Results:**
    *   If you get Esther: Keep the account and use the selector on **Chiwon**.
    *   If you get a standard 5-star: Use the selector to complement that unit's element.
    *   If you get nothing: Go to settings, delete the account (note the 30-day cooldown on the same email), and start over with a fresh guest ID.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Account deletion has a 30-day cooldown for the specific email address used. To reroll effectively in 2026, it is recommended to use "salted" emails or different login providers (Google, Apple, Guest).

## FAQ

**Q: Does the pity carry over if I don't get the featured character?**

A: Yes, the **mongil stardive summon strategy** relies heavily on the fact that pity carries over between limited banners. If you do 50 pulls on Esther and don't get a 5-star, you will only need 40 more pulls on the next banner (like Nara) to hit hard pity.

**Q: Should I spend my Compasses on the Standard Banner?**

A: Generally, no. You should use the free standard tickets the game gives you for the standard pool and save your premium Compasses for limited-time units like Esther, Nara, or Mina, who are often significantly more powerful.

**Q: Is Esther a "must-pull" for free-to-play players?**

A: She is not a must-pull, but she is very strong. If you prefer the Fire/Ice playstyle involving Nara and Flare, skipping Esther is a viable strategy to ensure you have enough currency for the next two major banners.

**Q: How many characters are in a standard team?**

A: Mongil: Star Dive features a 3-man team system. Unlike some other gacha games that use four characters, you only need to focus on building three highly synergistic units to reach peak efficiency.
