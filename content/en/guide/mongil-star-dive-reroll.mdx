## Advanced Reroll Strategies: The "Sweaty" Method

For players who want the absolute best start possible, some choose to ignore the beginner banner initially and focus on the **Weapon Banners**. The logic here is that weapons are harder to come by than characters.

In this strategy, you use your initial pulls to try and snag a 5-star signature weapon. After obtaining a weapon, you then use the guaranteed 5-star character selector (which unlocks after certain milestones) to pick the character that matches that weapon. This ensures you have a perfectly synchronized "BiS" (Best-in-Slot) setup from the start. However, this is significantly more difficult and time-consuming than a standard **mongil star dive reroll**.

> **💡 Tip:** If you pull Jiwon, you are set for the long term. She is an elemental support who will remain relevant even as newer, stronger DPS characters are released.

## FAQ

**Q: How long does a typical mongil star dive reroll take?**

A: If you skip all cutscenes and play on the easiest difficulty (Path of Dawn), a single reroll cycle takes about 10 to 12 minutes.

**Q: Can I get Esther from the beginner banner?**

A: No. Esther is a limited-time character and can only be obtained from her specific Limited Recruitment banner using "Compasses of Destiny." The beginner banner only contains the six standard 5-star characters (Jiwon, Flare, Sangun, Ophelia, Gabi, and Penny).

**Q: Should I stop rerolling once I get any 5-star?**

A: Since the beginner banner guarantees a 5-star, you should aim for a specific high-tier character like Jiwon or Flare. Settling for a lower-tier 5-star might make the early game slightly harder, though all 5-stars are viable for main story content.

**Q: Is there a pity system in Mongil: Star Dive?**

A: Yes. The standard and limited banners typically have a soft pity that starts around the 76th pull, with a hard guarantee at 90. However, the beginner banner is a one-time discounted offer that guarantees a 5-star within 16 pulls.
