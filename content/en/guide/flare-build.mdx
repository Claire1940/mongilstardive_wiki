The release of Temple has introduced a revolutionary rhythm-based playstyle to the Origin System, and perfecting your **flare build** is essential for any Tenno looking to push into high-level endurance runs. Temple utilizes a unique metronome mechanic that rewards players for timing their ability casts to the beat, culminating in the devastating power of the Flare (also known as Lizzie), an exalted flamethrower that melts through armor with ease. This specific **flare build** focuses on maximizing Heat proc damage while maintaining absolute invincibility through clever shield gating and ability timing. By understanding how to balance ability strength with the frame's internal rhythm, you can transform Temple into a walking supernova capable of clearing entire rooms in seconds.

## Core Mechanics of the Flare Build

Temple's power comes from the synergy between the frame's passive and its third ability, Ripper’s Whale. When using a **flare build**, the goal is to maintain a massive Heat damage buff that can scale up to 750%. This buff directly impacts the Flare exalted weapon, turning its beam into a high-status monster.

Survivability in this build is not handled through traditional armor or health tanking. Instead, it relies on the invincibility frames provided by Ripper’s Whale and the instant overshield restoration it offers. When you cast your third ability on the beat, you become completely invulnerable for a short duration, and your shields are instantly maxed out. This makes Redirection a surprisingly vital component of the setup, providing a massive buffer that synergizes with Arcane Aegis.

## Modding the Flare Exalted Weapon (Lizzie)

The Flare itself (Lizzie) functions as a beam weapon, similar to the Ignis Wraith but with significantly higher scaling potential. Because it does not use traditional ammo, you can focus entirely on fire rate and status application. Sinister Reach is a "must-have" for this **flare build**, as the base range of the beam can feel restrictive in large tilesets.

For the elemental configuration, while Heat is the primary focus, adding Magnetic is highly recommended for Steel Path. Magnetic allows the Flare to shred through Eximus Overguard in a matter of seconds, which is typically the only obstacle for a pure Heat-based weapon.

### Recommended Flare Weapon Mods:
1.  **Vile Acceleration**: Higher fire rate equals faster status stacking.
2.  **Sinister Reach**: Essential for beam length and ease of use.
3.  **Magnetic Capacity**: Specifically for bypassing Eximus Overguard.
4.  **Primary Merciless**: Provides consistent damage ramping without needing headshots.

## Advanced Gameplay Strategies

The secret to maximizing the **flare build** is staying "on tempo." The metronome in the center of the UI dictates your efficiency. Casting on the beat grants a 50% energy efficiency bonus, which allows you to run negative efficiency builds with Blind Rage comfortably. 

Furthermore, the Flare weapon has a unique interaction when aiming down sights (ADS). While ADS, your abilities change into elemental pulses. While these pulses are generally less efficient than the main beam for raw damage, they can be used to prime enemies with multiple status effects for Condition Overload-style scaling. However, for most general play, simply holding the trigger and burning everything in sight is the most effective path.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid subsuming over Ripper's Whale. It restricts you from using other damage buffs like Roar or Eclipse, but its defensive utility is too great to lose.

## Survivability in Steel Path

In the 2026 meta, Steel Path enemies on tilesets like Tav deal immense damage. The **flare build** handles this by ensuring that Temple is either invincible or shielded at all times. By using two Tauforged Casting Speed shards, you can reduce the animation time of Ripper's Whale, making the invincibility window more responsive. This allows you to react to shield breaks instantly, refreshing your 2,000+ overshields and becoming invulnerable before the enemies can bypass your gate.

## FAQ

**Q: Why use Redirection instead of Shield Gating with Brief Respite?**

A: Temple's third ability, Ripper's Whale, scales its overshield restoration based on your maximum shield capacity. By running Primed Redirection in your **flare build**, you reach over 2,000 shields instantly upon casting, which provides a much larger safety net than standard shield gating.

**Q: Does Archon Vitality need to be maxed out?**

A: No. The primary reason to include it in the **flare build** is the passive effect that doubles Heat procs. If you are low on Endo or Forma, an unranked version works perfectly fine as the health stat is rarely utilized due to Temple's invincibility mechanics.

**Q: Can I use Flare as my primary weapon?**

A: Yes, Flare is an exalted weapon, meaning it occupies its own slot and is summoned by Temple's fourth ability. It uses a unique "ability meter" as ammo, which you refill by casting your first and second abilities on the beat.

**Q: What is the best Archon Shard setup for this build?**

A: Two Amber shards for Casting Speed are highly recommended to make the ability rotations smoother. The remaining slots can be filled with Crimson shards for extra Ability Strength to further boost the Heat damage cap.
