## The Duplicate System: Seeds and Threads of Destiny

Pulling a duplicate character in the **mongil stardive gacha** is not a wasted effort. The game utilizes a "Breakthrough" or "Awakening" system that enhances a character's existing abilities or adds new utility to their kit. For example, awakening a character like Esther can significantly increase her stealth range or multiply her damage during specific skill windows.

When you obtain a duplicate five-star character, you receive specific materials used for these upgrades:

1.  **Seeds of Destiny:** These are the primary items required for character breakthroughs (2nd through 7th duplicates).
2.  **Threads of Destiny:** A secondary currency awarded alongside seeds. You receive 10 Threads for the 2nd through 7th duplicate, and 25 Threads for the 8th duplicate onwards.

The importance of these duplicates can vary between characters. While some units are fully functional at "Base" level (C0), others may see massive power spikes with their first or second awakening. Always check the character's "Awakened Passive" in the recruitment details to determine if pursuing duplicates is worth your premium currency.

## Soft Pity vs. Hard Pity Explained

Understanding the hidden math behind the **mongil stardive gacha** can save you a significant amount of money or in-game currency. The game employs a "climbing probability" system. 

For character banners, your chance remains 0.6% for pulls 1 through 75. On pull 76, the game adds a 6% bonus to your base rate. This means pull 76 has a 6.6% chance, pull 77 has a 12.6% chance, and so on. It is statistically very rare to actually reach the 90th pull, as the cumulative probability usually triggers a five-star drop between pulls 77 and 82.

For artifacts, this scaling begins at pull 66. Because the scaling is so aggressive (6% per pull), the artifact banner feels much more rewarding for players who are just shy of a full pity count.

## Monster Taming and the Monsterling System

While the gacha focuses on human characters and gear, the core of Mongil: Star Dive is the monsters. Players explore the world of Belana—specifically the continents of Centralia, The Far East, and The Deadlands—to capture and tame creatures.

*   **Monsterlings:** These are the essences of monsters. Even if a monster's physical form is exhausted, it remains alive as long as its Monsterling core is intact.
*   **The Monster Codex:** Capturing and training different species allows you to fill your Codex, which often provides account-wide bonuses or rewards.
*   **Link State:** Bound monsters can transform into their Monsterling forms at the owner's command, allowing for "tag-team" combat where you swap between characters and their monster companions.
