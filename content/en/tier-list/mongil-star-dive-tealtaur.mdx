## Combat Mechanics and Ability Breakdown

The fight against the **mongil star dive tealtaur** is divided into three distinct phases, each representing its descent into the abyss mentioned in the lore.

### Phase 1: The Shattered Balance
In the initial phase, the Tealtaur utilizes its massive physical frame to control the battlefield. It frequently uses "Dimensional Slam," an AoE attack that marks the ground with purple fractures. Players must move to the "harmony zones" created by support characters to avoid heavy damage.

### Phase 2: The Wound of the World
Once its HP reaches 60%, the Tealtaur opens a miniature rift. During this phase, it gains the "Resist Fate" buff, which grants it temporary invulnerability to ultimate abilities. You must destroy the three floating "Fate Shards" to break its shield.

### Phase 3: Chaos Unleashed
In the final 20% of the encounter, the **mongil star dive tealtaur** enters a berserk state. Its attack speed increases by 50%, and every hit applies a "Chaos Burn" debuff. This is a DPS race where players must utilize their "Star Dive" mechanic to finish the fight before the abyss consumes the arena.

|---|---|---|
| **Abyssal Roar** | Stuns all players for 3 seconds | Use a **Cleanse** potion or CC-immune skill |
| **Void Charge** | Linear dash dealing 400% damage | Dodge laterally when the red indicator appears |

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Mongil Star Dive Tealtaur?**

A: The Tealtaur is located in the "Rift of Aberration" zone, which becomes accessible after completing Chapter 7 of the main story questline. It is a recurring boss that can be farmed daily for evolution materials.

**Q: What is the best element to use against the Tealtaur?**

A: The **mongil star dive tealtaur** is weakest to the **Harmony** element. Using characters that can infuse their weapons with Harmony will significantly reduce the time required to break its "Fate Shield" in Phase 2.

**Q: Can I recruit Tealtaur as a playable monster?**

A: While you cannot recruit the boss version of Tealtaur directly, you can obtain a "Tealtaur Soul" through high-level Abyssal Summons. This smaller version retains the Abyss affinity and is excellent for PvP content.

**Q: Why does the Tealtaur look familiar to the characters in the lore video?**

A: Without spoiling too much of the 2026 story arc, the Tealtaur is a reflection of the "entwined destinies" mentioned in the ancient book. Its physical form is shaped by the memories of those who have previously resisted fate, which is why it bears a resemblance to ancient guardians of the world before the "wound" was created.
