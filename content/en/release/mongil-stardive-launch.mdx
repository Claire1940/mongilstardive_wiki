## The World of Belana: Lore and Continents

The setting of MONGIL: STAR DIVE is the world of Belana, a realm where humans, demihumans, and monsters exist in a delicate balance. The lore traces back to a creator deity known as "Bigcat," who is said to have woven the world from the Threads of Fate floating within a primordial void. This mythological foundation sets the stage for the conflict involving the "Wound of the World," an event that nearly wiped out the elves and led to the emergence of monsters.

Belana is divided into three primary continents, each offering distinct environments and challenges for Divers.

| Continent | Description | Key Locations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Centralia** | The starting point of the journey and heart of civilization. | Kingdom of Solismenamus |
| **The Far East** | A distant land across the sea with unique cultural aesthetics. | Baek-Ah |
| **The Deadlands** | A desolate region rendered uninhabitable by ancient events. | Unknown Ruins |

Exploring these regions is not just about combat; it is about uncovering the "magic stones" that define the monsters of this world. Every creature classified as a monster possesses one of these stones, which serves as the source of their power and intelligence.

## Understanding Monsterlings and Taming

One of the most innovative features highlighted during the **mongil stardive launch** is the "Monsterling" mechanic. In Belana, a monster is not truly dead as long as its core—the Monsterling—remains intact. This essence allows monsters to be bound to their owners and stored in a compact form.

### The Linking Process
When a monster is bound, it enters a "linked" state with the Diver. This allows the monster to transform into its Monsterling form at the owner's command, making it easy to transport and deploy in various combat scenarios. 

*   **Magic Stones:** The biological marker of all monsters.
*   **Intelligence:** Some monsters possess the ability to communicate, leading to both hostile and friendly interactions with demihumans.
*   **Reversion:** An interesting quirk of the world’s physics is that pouring hot water onto a Monsterling will cause it to revert to its original form immediately, even without a command.

| Mechanic | Function | Strategic Benefit |

| **Transformation** | Shifts the monster into its core essence. | Allows for rapid tactical switching. |
